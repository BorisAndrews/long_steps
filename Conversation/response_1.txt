For a symmetric positive definite matrix $A$, the gradient is $\nabla f(x) = Ax - b$. Assuming the optimal solution is $x^*$, the error at step $k$ is $e_k = x_k - x^*$. The iteration can then be written as $e_{k+1} = (I - h_k A) e_k$. After $n$ iterations, the relationship between the initial error and the final error is governed by a degree-$n$ polynomial of the matrix $A$:$$e_n = \left[ \prod_{k=0}^{n-1} (I - h_k A) \right] e_0 = p_n(A) e_0$$The polynomial $p_n(t) = \prod_{k=0}^{n-1} (1 - h_k t)$ is constrained such that $p_n(0) = 1$, and its roots are located at the reciprocals of the step sizes, $t_k = 1/h_k$.
